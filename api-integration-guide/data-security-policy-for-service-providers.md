# Data Security Policy for Service Providers

# 

Data Security Policy for Service Providers

This policy provides guidelines to help service providers enhance compliance and security in data processing, while preventing data breaches caused by both internal and external security threats. It is also aimed at strengthening system security and standardizing practices among service providers.

This policy applies to service providers receiving data shared by Temu. In the event of a violation, Temu may require corrective action and may suspend or terminate data access if necessary.

### 

**1.Definitions**

**Service Provider**: Refers to any external service provider receiving data from Temu, including logistics providers, ERP service providers, merchandise partners with self-developed applications, and others.

**Data subject**: Refers to any individual who can be identified, directly or indirectly.

**Personal Data**: Refers to any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual or household.

**Security Incident**: Refers to a security breach that results in the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to personal data that is transmitted, stored or otherwise processed.

### 

**2.General Security Measures**

Service Providers shall implement the following security measures to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data and to ensure that the organization's operations comply with industry best practices and applicable regulatory requirements.

#### 

** 1）Personnel Requirements**

Service Providers shall appoint dedicated personnel responsible for overseeing data security.

- 

Service Providers shall conduct audits and risk assessments to evaluate the likelihood and impact of data security risks. These assessments help monitor and ensure compliance with established policies and procedures. In addition, senior management shall to review the results of both audits and assessments.

- 

Service Providers shall provide annual data security and personal data protection training to their staff to raise awareness and ensure ongoing compliance.

#### 

** 2）Data Classification and Handling**

Service Providers shall classify organizational data based on its confidentiality, availability, and integrity requirements, defining appropriate safeguards for each classification level. In particular, sensitive data should be segregated to maintain its security.

#### 

** 3）Access Control**

- 

Service Providers shall implement role-based access control (RBAC) and adhere to the principles of need-to-know and least privilege. Access to data and system functions will be restricted to what is essential for users based on their specific roles and responsibilities.

- 

Service Providers shall regularly review and adjust access privileges to ensure that access privileges are revoked or modified within 24 hours of employment termination or significant changes in job responsibilities.

- 

Service Providers shall monitor and document all unauthorized access attempts to ensure a prompt response and enhance security measures.

#### 

** 4）Password and Key Management**

- 

Use of unique IDs and passwords for all users.

- 

Adhere to industry standards for password complexity, including requiring a minimum of 8 characters, a mix of special characters, and both uppercase and lowercase letters.

- 

Ensure encrypted storage of all passwords and keys. Prohibit the use of plaintext passwords in code or databases to prevent unauthorized access.

- 

Update passwords regularly and implement a password history policy to prevent the reuse of recent passwords, thereby strengthening security protection against potential breaches.

- 

Require immediate password changes after initial use to enhance security.

#### 

** 5）Encryption in Transmission**

Service Providers shall encrypt all Information in transmission with secure protocols such as TLS 1.2, TLS 1.2+, SFTP, etc.

#### 

** 6）Deletion and Destruction**

- 

Service Providers shall delete Temu's data within 30 days of receiving Temu's notice, unless otherwise required by applicable laws or regulations.

- 

Deletion shall follow industry best practices, including but not limited to both logical and physical deletion methods.

- 

Service Providers shall comply with Temu’s requirements to certify that all information has been securely destroyed.

#### 

** 7）Security Monitoring**

- 

Service Provides shall implement technical measures to monitor and record network operations and cybersecurity incidents, with related logs retained in accordance with requirements.

- 

Unless explicitly required by laws and regulations, logs should not contain any Personal Data.

- 

Service Providers shall establish change management procedures and tracking mechanisms designed to test, approve and monitor all material changes to the Service Provider’s technology and information assets.

- 

Service Providers shall conduct thorough review and approvals, and continuously monitor technology and information assets to ensure security and compliance.

#### 

** 8）Cyber Security**

- 

Service Providers shall implement and maintain network security controls that provide for the use of enterprise firewalls and layered DMZ architectures, and intrusion detection systems and other traffic and event correlation procedures designed to protect systems from intrusion and limit the scope of any successful attack.

- 

Service Providers shall implement and maintain vulnerability assessment, patch management and threat protection technologies, and scheduled monitoring procedures designed to identify, assess, mitigate and protect against identified security threats, viruses and other malicious code.

#### 

** 9）Physical and Environmental Security**

Service Providers shall ensure the physical and environmental security of data centers, server room facilities, and other areas where Personal Data is stored:

  - 

Protect information assets from unauthorized physical access.

  - 

Manage, monitor, and record all personnel entering and exiting secure facilities.

  - 

Prevent environmental hazards, such as high temperatures, fire, and water damage.

#### 

** 10）Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery**

Service Providers shall implement business resiliency/continuity and disaster recovery procedures designed to maintain service and/or recovery from foreseeable emergencies or disasters.

#### 

** 11）Security Incident**

- 

Service Providers shall establish comprehensive procedures for reporting and handling data Security Incidents, clearly defining roles, responsibilities, and specific steps for on-site incident handling, incident reporting, and post-incident recovery.

- 

Service Providers shall notify Temu within 24 hours of detecting any Security Incident.

### 

**3.Additional Requirements for Personal Data**

If the service provided involves the transfer of Personal Data from Temu, the following additional requirements must be met:

#### 

** 1）Service Providers shall publish and regularly update an internal Personal Data protection policy.**

#### 

** 2）Data Encryption**

- 

Service Providers shall use industry-standard encryption technologies for transferring and storing Personal Data, especially when transmitted over public networks (e.g., the Internet), by wireless methods, or stored on portable or removable media (e.g., laptops, CDs/DVDs, USB drives, backup tapes).

- 

Service Providers shall encrypt all Personal Data (including but not limited to name, address, e-mail address, phone number, ID, etc.) using cryptographic methods that complies with applicable laws, regulations, or best practices. The use of DES, DES3, MD5, SHA-1, or RSA with a key size smaller than 2048 is prohibited.

#### 

** 3）Data Retention**

- 

Service Providers shall delete Personal Data within 30 days upon completion of the service.

- 

Service Providers may retain Personal Data for over 30 days upon completion of the service only if required by applicable law, and solely for the purpose of complying with that law.

- 

Data Subject Rights

- 

Service Providers must assist Temu in fulfilling Data Subject rights.

#### 

** 4）Cross-border Data Transfer**

The transfer of Personal Data across jurisdictions must comply with the legal and regulatory requirements of both the originating and receiving jurisdictions, and  relevant compliance documentations must be provided to Temu.

### 

**4.Audit**

** 1）**Temu may conduct an audit either directly or through an independent certified public accounting firm, which may take place at the Service Provider’s facilities and/or those of its subcontractor.

** 2）**Service Providers shall maintain all relevant records necessary for the audit throughout the duration of this agreement and for 12 months thereafter.

** 3）**If the audit reveals any violation of this Policy or applicable laws and regulations, the Service Provider shall, at its own cost and expense, remediate those risks within an agreed-upon time frame.

### 

**5.Others**

This policy is provided in both English and Chinese. In the event of any discrepancies, the English version shall prevail.
